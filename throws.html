Basic Throws





Backhand

  1. Put the four fingers of your dominant hand in the cup of the disc with your thumb on top.
  2. If you are a righty, stand straight up, twist to the left, bend your wrist and elbow in front of you so they are in front of the disc.
  3. Step foward, un-twist and release the disc.
  4. Try to keep the disc as level as possible and to give it spin.
  5. Remember that most of the spin comes from bending your wrist then snapping it.

    6. As you get better you will use less wind-up and less extension. Backhand can be used for short, medium, and long-range passing.


Forehand(or 'Flick')

    This throw takes some practice and commitment.


  1. Put 2 fingers (Pointer & middle) under the disc and your thumb on top. Throw as if you were trying to skip a flat rock on water.
  2. Keep the disc parallel with the ground when you release.
  3. Pull your wrist as far back as it will go.
  4. Snap your wrist forward while bringing your arm inward and release.
  5. The disc should wrap around and “flick” off your two fingers that are under the frisbee.

    6. It will take a few tries to perfect. Be patient with yourself! This throw is really good for short, medium, and long range.


Hammer

  1. Hold the disc with your pointer and middle finger on the inner side of the disc, your thumb on the other side. (It is typically held the same way as a Flick.
  2. The top of the disc should be facing your left side.
  3. Tilt the top of the disc slightly to the left side to give it an angle.
  4. Pull back and release the disc when it gets even with your forehead, keep your elbow bent at all times.
  5. If done right the disc should start out tilted and slowly turn upside-down.
  6. The disc should land completely upside down on its face because it makes it more predictable and easier to catch for you teammate.
